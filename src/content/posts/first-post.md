## Introduction
AwesomeUser89 spends his Friday evenings ‘debating’ with viewers on Tiktok that an omniscient God exists.
Meanwhile, Bulky_Coder9326 is making his 6th video ‘showing’ how the mind and the body are related.
Finally, PromiscuousThinker has sent his AI-generated paper, proving that the Many-Worlds Interpretation is the correct Quantum Mechanics theory, to five professors (none of them will write back). 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You may not know it, especially by the authority and persuasiveness with which these guys seem to present, but these are some of the biggest, unsolved problems in philosophy and science today. And therein lies the problem. Nowadays, as I browse philosophical and scientific comment sections, online forums, conversation spaces, the discussions taking place seem…definitive. By “definitive”, I mean that everyone appears to have resolved all of the many difficult and complex problems that plague us, despite there being no consensus-forming concrete evidence. From questions dealing with the origins of the universe to those that query the functions of consciousness, there seems to be, at least to me, a lack of (what I'm calling) “in-progress thinking”. 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“In-progress thinking”, as the name suggests, refers to deliberating deeply on a problem without reaching a definitive conclusion or coming to a final resolution,  i.e. your thinking doesn’t finish, it's continuous it's “in-progress”. Now, you may ask “Well, why would we ever want this? Isn't the goal, of deep contemplation, to find answers?” to which I respond: It absolutely is, but the problem is specific to these complicated, unresolved philosophical and scientific queries that lack both empirical support and ways to obtain that support. In these cases, your answers aren’t grounded in sufficient evidence or demonstrable fact and are merely intuitive, well-reasoned guesses. Or, in other words, they're simply opinions. And, I observe that once most reach these opinions, they are treating their opinions on these questions as the ultimate truth, (even though for these questions, ̶a̶l̶l̶  most opinions are equally plausible) and refusing to consider other perspectives or allow their views to be updated...essentially, their thinking has concluded and that's the real problem (the issue isn't having opinions, it's treating them as immutable when evidence is scarce).

### “So, what’s the alternative?”
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honestly speaking, I don't believe we should waste time trying to change these types of people as it’s very unlikely that these people can be persuaded to change (thus, it’s also highly likely that those spaces will remain as they are). However, what we (i.e. people who share the same sentiment as me) can do is create our OWN space to discuss, deliberate and NOT reach conclusions on these issues. Hence, I introduce this blog. I'm not here to give you hot takes (well, not ALL the time). I'm thinking this through in real-time, and I'm inviting you to join me in that. Unlike most blogs, my essays (for the most part) will not be me presenting my viewpoints as the ‘correct opinion” on a particular subject and trying to persuade readers. Instead, you'll see me explore these ideas further, dig deeper, ask more questions than I answer, because that's how we (and our knowledge) progress further, and develop greater: by pausing, reflecting, and deconstructing. I am excited (and nervous) for the road ahead. There are a lot of ideas, ranging from All things quantum to Math to Philosophy and even politics, that I want to discuss, the first of which is already up, and I do hope you will join me in those discussions (as without you, it wouldn't even be a discussion). Looking forward to those discussions and where it takes us!


You might be wondering who's behind these musings, but let's remember, we're here to engage with ideas, not credentials. (Though, I'll satisfy curiosity in due time.😉) <!--For now, the better question (and one I suspect you will be asking a lot) is “Is this person slow?”-->
